What is mental illness?

Mental illness, also called mental health disorders, refers to a wide range of mental health conditions/disorders that affect your mood, thinking and behavior.
Examples of mental illness include depression, anxiety disorders, schizophrenia, eating disorders and addictive behaviors.

What are some of the effects of the mental disorders?

Unaddressed mental health problems can have a negative influence on homelessness, poverty, employment, safety, and the local economy. They may impact the productivity of local businesses and health care costs,
impede the ability of children and youth to succeed in school, and lead to family and community disruption.Other effects include

  • Changes in mood
  • Eratic thinking
  • Chronic anxiety
  • Exaggeratedsense of self-worth
  • Impulsive actions

ANXIETY

Anxiety is your body’s natural response to stress. It’s a feeling of fear or apprehension about what’s to come. The first day of being in a new environment eg going to a job interview, giving a speech may cause most people to feel fearful and nervous.

Symptoms

  • Feeling faint or dizzy
  • Dry mouth
  • Shortness of breath
  • Sweating
  • Apprehension and worry
  • Chills or hot flashes
  • Restlessness
  • Fear

An Anxiety attack

An anxiety attack is a feeling of overwhelming apprehension, worry, distress, or fear. For many people, an anxiety attack builds slowly. It may worsen as a stressful event approaches.

Symptoms

  • Impaired judgment
  • Feeing wired
  • Sleeping little but not feeling tired
  • A sense of distraction or boredom
  • Missing work or school
  • Underperforming at work or school
  • Feeling able to do anything

BIPOLAR DISORDER

People with bipolar disorder will experience changes in mood, energy, and activity levels that can make day-to-day living difficult.

SCHIZOPHRENIA

Schizophrenia is a serious mental disorder in which people interpret reality abnormally. Schizophrenia may result in some combination of hallucinations, delusions, and extremely disordered thinking and behavior that impairs daily functioning, and can be disabling.

Common Causes are:

It's not known what causes schizophrenia, but researchers believe that a combination of genetics, brain chemistry and environment contributes to development of the disorder.
